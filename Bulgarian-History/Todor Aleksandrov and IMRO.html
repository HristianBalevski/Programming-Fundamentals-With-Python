TODOR ALEKSANDROV AND IMRO

Todor Aleksandrov Poprusev was born on March 4, 1881 in Novo Selo - neighborhood of the city of Stip.
In 1898, at the age of 17, he enters the ranks of IMRO. After the Ilinden Uprising (1903) of january 1905,
he was made district leader in the Skopje area. Since 1911, he was a member of the Central Committee(HQ)
of VMRO.

He was actively involved in the Balkan War from 1912-1913 and the World War from 1915-1918, with
the organization of detachments and troops, who fought alongside the Bulgarian Army.

By 1922, the organization is dealing with rampant delinquency in Pirin Macedonia and in fact, VMRO becomes
a "state in a state". After 1920 under his leadership, IMRO resumed its revolutionary activities in Vardar
and Aegean Macedonia. Todor Aleksandrov becomes an insurmountable factor in the politics of Bulgaria
and The Balkans. Even during the course of his lifetime, Encyclopedia Britannica calls him "the uncrowned king
of the bulgarians in Macedonia."

On August 31, 1924 Todor Aleksandrov was killed in the Pirin Mountainon the site called Flat Beech ("Ravna Buka").
The management of the organization decided to bury him here, in the chapel of St.Elias.
